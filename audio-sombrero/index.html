Audio Sombrero

Hardware

Overview

This sombrero plays Mexican music when you put in on!

In this project, a momentary capacitive touch sensor is used to detect human touch, in this case your head, when the sombrero is put on. The Audio FX Sound Board is triggered by the sensor to play a random song that has been saved onto the board's 16MB flash storage. Ogg Vorbis Mono Output format audio files were uploaded to maximize the number of songs stored. Nine songs were fit this way, all of which are off the Original Soundtrack of a video game called Guacamelee. The moment I heard these songs I knew I had to walk around with them playing from my head. If the sombrero is still on once a song has finished playing, it will begin playing one of the remaining songs at random, and you can get through all 9 songs this way. Otherwise, it will not play another song until you put the sombrero back on. The audio board outputs to a battery powered speaker that can get impressively loud as you can see in the video below. The sombrero also acts as a resonator due to its shape, and amplifies the audio, so it's even louder once mounted to the sombrero. The audio in the video is produced by the sombrero.
