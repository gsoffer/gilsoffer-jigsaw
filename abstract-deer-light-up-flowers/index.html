Abstract Deer - Light Up Flowers

Hardware

Overview

In this project, I add LED backing to my "Abstract Deer" painting to produce a whimsical pattern of periodically changing randomly lit flowers, controlled by the Adafruit Trinket microcontroller

This painting features a deer with pink flowers stemming from its antlers. NeoPixel LEDs were added in the rear of the canvas, behind the individual flowers. Six flowers are lit at any given time, with one flower fading out and a different flower lighting up every three seconds. This is controlled by the Trinket microcontroller. The project is powered by a massive 20,000 mAh LiPo power bank for extended use without needing to recharge. Two variations of the code were used, one with all LEDs pink, and one with a randomly chosen color for each lit LED. Both are nice, but I prefer all LEDs pink. Also, since the flowers in the painting are pink, the "random colors" still have a pink hue, and so it must be fairly dark to be able distinguish their true colors.
