Overview

A globe that can be spoken to and navigated, all powered by the Raspberry Pi

When spoken the name of a city, state, or country, in the world, the globe moves to shine a laser at that location, and then reads aloud corresponding demographic information about it. Audio is recorded while the push-button is pressed down. A USB microphone is used for audio input, and USB powered speakers are used for audio output. Additionally, a laser diode is used to point at the appropriate location. Three servo motors are used together for longitudinal motion, for a full 360 degree sweep. And one additional motor is used for latitude. The motors have less than 180 degrees of motion, so the arm can't sweep completely to the north or south poles using one latitudinal motor, but this isn't a problem because the only location affected is Antarctica, and the arm still sweeps far enough to hit the broader continent. Also, in order to account for the globe's inertia (it will not actually move if the longitude changes by less than 15 degrees), the globe moves 80 degrees to the left or right of the final position first, and then moves to the correct position, gaining momentum as it moves to the correct location.
