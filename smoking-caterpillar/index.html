Overview

Become Alice in Wonderland's Smoking Caterpillar, and animate your words using the Raspberry Pi

In Disney's "Alice in Wonderland" (1951), as the Smoking Caterpillar smokes from his hookah, the words he speaks take life in the form of smoke, shaped as letters and objects. In this project, a microphone is attached to the end of a hookah hose. When specific words or phrases (those spoken in the film) are spoken, the LED matrix displays corresponding colorful animations. This project uses a Raspberry Pi, which performs the speech recognition using Carnegie Mellon University's open source "pocketsphinx" software. This project is programmed in Python, and bitmaps are drawn using the ImageDraw module. Each animation is created by displaying a bitmap for a short amount of time, then redrawing a part of the bitmap, and flashing the new, slightly modified, bitmap to the matrix. Redrawing bitmaps this way is used to produce smooth and complex animations on the matrix. The Adafruit Pi LED Matrix Hat is used to flash the bitmaps to the 32x64 LED matrix. This project was extremely fun to work on because I had complete creative freedom in coming up with the animations. And it was similarly an exciting challenge to come up with clever and efficient ways to program these animations once I decided on them. As opposed to contemplating what sorts of motions would be possible / easy to program and then choosing animations based on this, the approach I took was to leave my imagination unrestricted and choose animations that I felt were comical, peculiar, or hypnotic, which was a rewarding, challenging, and educational process.
